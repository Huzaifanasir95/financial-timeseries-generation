Statistical Methods

Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test

Measures maximum difference between cumulative distributions. Lower scores indicate better distributional similarity. Thresholds: KS < 0.1 (excellent), 0.1-0.3 (good), 0.3-0.5 (fair).

Paired T-Test

Tests null hypothesis that paired samples have equal means. P-value < 0.05 indicates significant difference at 95% confidence level. Used for comparing TimeGAN vs Diffusion.

Cohen's d (Effect Size)

Standardized measure of effect size: |d| < 0.2 (small), 0.2-0.5 (medium), 0.5-0.8 (large),> 0.8 (very large). Our d=-2.21 indicates TimeGAN substantially outperforms Diffusion.

Mean Difference

Absolute difference between real and synthetic feature means. Lower values indicate better preservation of central tendency. Critical for financial data where accurate mean returns matter.
