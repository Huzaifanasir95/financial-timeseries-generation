Diffusion Model Mathematics

1. Forward Diffusion Process

Gradually adds Gaussian noise over T=1000 timesteps:

q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = N(x_t; √(1-β_t)·x_{t-1}, β_t·I)

Where β_t is the variance schedule

2. Direct Sampling at Timestep t

Can sample directly at any timestep:

q(x_t | x_0) = N(x_t; √ᾱ_t·x_0, (1-ᾱ_t)·I)

Where ᾱ_t = ∏(1-β_s) for s=1 to t

3. Reverse Denoising Process

Learned neural network reverses the noise:

p_θ(x_{t-1} | x_t) = N(x_{t-1}; μ_θ(x_t,t), Σ_θ(x_t,t))

Neural network predicts the mean μ_θ

4. Mean Prediction Formula

Compute mean from predicted noise:

μ_θ(x_t,t) = (1/√α_t)·(x_t - ((1-α_t)/√(1-ᾱ_t))·ε_θ(x_t,t))

ε_θ is the predicted noise from Transformer

Simplified Training Loss
L_simple = E[||ε - ε_θ(x_t, t)||²]

MSE between actual and predicted noise
